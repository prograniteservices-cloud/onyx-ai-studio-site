# Product Design Document

## Product
Onyx AI Studio is a founder-led AI development studio website for high-trust freelance and agency lead generation.

## Audience
- Operators at small and mid-sized businesses who need practical automation.
- Retail, service, and content-heavy businesses that need search, workflows, or modern web systems.
- Buyers comparing a nimble AI studio against generic developers or agencies.

## Positioning
Onyx AI Studio builds practical AI systems, automation workflows, and fast web platforms for real business operations. The public message should emphasize useful systems, clear implementation, and visible proof without exposing private personal details.

## Primary Conversion Goals
- Request a project fit review.
- Explore service pages.
- Read case studies.
- Understand the studio's capability in AI integration, automation, web development, and SEO content systems.

## Core Pages
- `/`: proof-led landing page.
- `/services`: service overview.
- `/services/ai-integration`: AI integration service page.
- `/services/automation-workflows`: automation service page.
- `/services/web-development`: web development service page.
- `/services/seo-content-systems`: SEO and content systems page.
- `/case-studies`: case study index.
- `/case-studies/vapeos`: AI retail inventory search case study.
- `/case-studies/starry`: ambient companion app case study.
- `/case-studies/unicorn-island`: interactive learning/game case study.
- `/case-studies/regional-service-site`: regional service SEO site case study.
- `/insights`: thought leadership index.
- `/insights/ai-inventory-semantic-search`: foundational insight article.
- `/contact`: lead form and fit criteria.

## CTA Strategy
- Primary CTA: "Request a fit review".
- Secondary CTA: "View case studies".
- Contact form CTA: "Request my fit review".
- Keep CTAs outcome-focused and restrained.

## Content Rules
- No public personal names, addresses, private contact details, or source context from private parent-context files.
- Case studies can describe systems and outcomes, but avoid client-private facts unless already intended for portfolio use.
- Use specific capabilities and proof points where safe.
